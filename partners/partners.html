Our Partners

Meow meow meow meow...

What it sounds like when the cats greet us in the morning!

Paws for a Cuppa works with local cat rescues in order to promote adoptions of cats and kittens. Although our main cast of cats are permanently adopted at the cafe, from time to time we will foster adoptable cats. Being at the cafe helps young cats and kittens to learn how to interact with humans, and provides them with much-needed exercise! If you find yourself bonding with one of our adoptable cats, please ask the staff for information about adopting.

Paws for a Cuppa also runs regular educational and awareness events, where experts from our partner institutions answer cat-related questions and help squash commonly held myths about cat ownership. Check out our calendar for the next upcoming event!

Partner Websites

The following rescues are just some of the many organizations which do amazing rescue work in and around the Ottawa area -- please check them out! Click here to expand descriptions.

  • Ottawa Stray Cat Rescue
    • "[A]n Ottawa based rescue group attempting to home stray cats in Ottawa, and run a trap-neuter-return program for community cats."
  • Furry Tales Cat Rescue
    • "Furry Tales is a foster based agency located in Smiths Falls Ontario, charitable status registration number 791529910RR0001.
    • Our primary goal is to assist cats (older and middle aged) who have been displaced, abandoned or at high risk of being euthanized in the Quebec pound system - an antiquated system that sees 75% of all cats intake euthanized within 2 weeks of arrival."
  • Hungry and Homeless Cat Rescue
    • "HHCR is an animal rescue organization in Ottawa, Ontario. Our goal is to save injured or found cats and try to find them permanent suitable homes. Medical help is arranged for any cat suffering from injuries or illness. All cats are at temporary foster homes until adopted. We rely on volunteers and donations from kind individuals who care."
  • Lanark Animal Welfare Society
    • LAWS is an independent no-kill organization dedicated to promoting the well-being of all animals. It is a registered charity that survives on private donations, and receives no government funding. Every year the shelter staff deals with many animals. These are lost, abused, or abandoned animals which are cared for until their rightful owners can be found or they can be placed in new homes.
